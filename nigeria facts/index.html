Nigerian Fact File

Discover the wonders of Nigeria

Nigerian Flag

The Nigerian flag was adopted in 1960. It features three vertical bands: green on the left, white in the middle, and green on the right. The green represents agriculture and wealth, while white represents peace and unity.

Three Major Tribes of Nigeria

  • Hausa - Predominantly in Northern Nigeria
  • Yoruba - Predominantly in Southwestern Nigeria
  • Igbo - Predominantly in Southeastern Nigeria

Current President

Nigeria's Current Population

~218 million

Nigeria is the most populous country in Africa and the 6th most populous country in the world.

Number of States

36 States

Nigeria has 36 states plus the Federal Capital Territory (FCT), Abuja.

Capital of Nigeria

Abuja

Abuja became the capital in 1991, replacing Lagos. It is located in the center of the country.

Oldest University in Nigeria

University of Ibadan

Established in 1948, the University of Ibadan is the first university in Nigeria.

Official Language

English Language

English is the official language of Nigeria, inherited from British colonial rule. However, there are over 500 indigenous languages spoken across the country.

Major Religions in Nigeria

  • Islam - Predominantly in Northern Nigeria
  • Christianity - Predominantly in Southern Nigeria
  • Traditional Religions - Practiced in various communities
