The Sevenfold Gifts

According to Isaiah 11:2-3 and the Catechism of the Catholic Church

👑

Wisdom

Sapientia

The first and highest gift of the Holy Spirit. It enables us to see all things in relation to God and to judge all things according to God's perspective.

"The fear of the Lord is the beginning of wisdom" — Proverbs 9:10
Featured Saint: St. Thomas Aquinas
  • Discernment
  • Deep understanding
  • God-centered perspective
💡

Understanding

Gift II
💡

Understanding

Intellectus

Helps us comprehend the truths of our faith and apply them to our daily life. It gives insight into the meaning of Scripture and Church teachings.

"He who teaches man knowledge" — Isaiah 28:26
Featured Saint: St. Augustine
  • Comprehension of faith
  • Insight into Scripture
  • Application of truth

Counsel

Consilium

Provides supernatural guidance in difficult moral situations. It helps us know what God wants us to do and gives us the courage to follow His will.

"I will guide you in the way of wisdom" — Proverbs 4:11
Featured Saint: St. Joseph
  • Moral guidance
  • Right judgment
  • Direction in decisions

Fortitude

Fortitudo

Gives us the courage to stand firm in our faith, even in persecution. It strengthens us to overcome difficulties and remain faithful to God's commands.

"Be strong and courageous" — Deuteronomy 31:6
Featured Saint: St. Thomas More
  • Courage in trials
  • Perseverance
  • Spiritual strength

"The Lord gives knowledge" — 2 Samuel 22:36
Featured Saint: St. Albert the Great
  • Understanding creation
  • Divine truth
  • Spiritual insight

Piety

Pietas

"Serve the Lord with fear" — Psalm 2:11
Featured Saint: St. Francis of Assisi
  • Reverence
  • Filial love
  • Devoted worship

Fear of the Lord

Timor Domini

"The fear of the Lord is the beginning of knowledge" — Proverbs 1:7

  • Protection from sin
  • Spiritual reverence

Saints Who Exemplified the Gifts

Holy men and women who lived these gifts to the fullest

📖

St. Thomas Aquinas

1225-1274

The "Angel of the Schools" embodied Wisdom through his profound theological writings. His Summa Theologica remains a masterpiece of Catholic doctrine.

Wisdom
🎓

St. Augustine

354-430

His "Confessions" show deep Understanding of human nature and divine grace. He transformed Western theology with his insight into God's love.

Understanding
🏠

St. Joseph

1st Century

The earthly father of Jesus received divine Counsel in dreams, guiding him to protect the Holy Family through difficult circumstances.

Counsel
⚖️

St. Thomas More

1478-1535

Martyr for the Faith, he showed Fortitude by choosing death over compromising his conscience against Henry VIII's demands.

Fortitude
🔬

St. Albert the Great

1206-1280

The "Universal Doctor" had remarkable Knowledge of both natural sciences and theology, being a mentor to Thomas Aquinas.

Knowledge
🕊️

St. Francis of Assisi

1181-1226

His life of radical Piety and love for all creation transformed the Church and inspired millions to follow Christ's poverty.

Piety
⛪

St. John Vianney

1786-1859

The Curé of Ars embodied holy Fear of the Lord, spending 17 hours daily in prayer and hearing confessions.

Fear of the Lord

Cultivate Your Gifts

Practical ways to grow in each divine gift

📿

Wisdom

  • Pray daily for divine guidance
  • Read and meditate on Scripture
  • Seek counsel from spiritual directors
  • Practice discernment in decisions
  • Study the teachings of the Church
📖

Understanding

  • Study the Catechism regularly
  • Reflect on Sunday's Gospel
  • Ask questions about your faith
  • Discuss theology with others
  • Read spiritual classics
🗣️

Counsel

  • Develop a spiritual director relationship
  • Join a faith formation group
  • Pray for guidance before decisions
  • Listen to wise counsel
  • Trust your informed conscience
💪

Fortitude

  • Pray for courage in difficulties
  • Face your fears with faith
  • Support others in trials
  • Practice penance and self-discipline
  • Stand up for moral truths
🌍

Knowledge

  • Study God's creation in nature
  • Learn about Catholic tradition
  • Appreciate science and faith
  • See God in all things
  • Continue lifelong learning
🙏

Piety

  • Pray the Liturgy of the Hours
  • Receive sacraments frequently
  • Practice reverence in worship
  • Serve the poor generously
  • Cultivate family prayer
敬畏

Fear of the Lord

  • Maintain awareness of God's presence
  • Practice frequent confession
  • Meditate on Last Things
  • Avoid near occasions of sin
  • Live with eternal perspective

Prayers to the Holy Spirit

Invocate the Divine Gifts in your life

Come, Holy Spirit

Come, Holy Spirit, fill my heart with Your divine gifts.

Grant me the gift of Wisdom to see all things in Your light.
Bestow upon me Understanding to know Your truth.
Give me Counsel to guide my steps aright.
Strengthen me with Fortitude in every trial.
Grant me Knowledge of Your creation.
Fill me with Piety to worship You worthily.
Keep me in Fear of Your holy name.

Amen.

Prayer for the Seven Gifts

O God, who by the light of the Holy Spirit,
did instruct the hearts of the faithful,
grant that by the same Holy Spirit
we may be truly wise and ever rejoice in His consolations.

Through Christ our Lord.

Amen.

Novena to the Holy Spirit

Holy Spirit, divine Consoler,
I adore You as my true God.
I believe in You because You are the Divine Physician,
healer of my soul.

Come and cleanse me, heal my wounds, sin and suffering.
You are my forgiveness, my hope, my peace, my light.
Teach me to pray aright.

Amen.

Veni Creator Spiritus

Latin Traditional

Come, Creator Spirit,

Come, Holy Spirit, Creator blest,
And in our souls take up Thy rest;
Come with Thy grace and heavenly aid
To fill the hearts which Thou hast made.

Great Paraclete! to Thee we cry,
O highest gift of God most high!
O fount of life! O fire of love!
And sweet anointing from above!

Amen.

Anima Christi

Soul of Christ

Soul of Christ, sanctify me.
Body of Christ, save me.
Blood of Christ, inebriate me.
Water from the side of Christ, wash me.
Passion of Christ, strengthen me.
O good Jesus, hear me.
Within Thy wounds, hide me.
From departing, keep me.
From the enemy, defend me.
At the hour of death, call me.
And bid me come to Thee,
That with Thy saints I may praise Thee
Forever and ever.

Amen.

Holy Spirit, My Comforter

Holy Spirit, my Comforter,
I ask You to fill me with Your presence.
You are the gift of God,
The source of all good gifts.

Grant me the gifts of the Spirit:
Wisdom to know God's will,
Understanding to comprehend His truth,
Counsel to make right choices,
Fortitude to persevere in faith,
Knowledge to grow in grace,
Piety to love and serve,
And holy Fear to remain close to God.

Amen.

Sequence for Pentecost

Veni, Sancte Spiritus

Come, Holy Spirit, come!

And from Thy celestial home
Shed a ray of light divine!

Come, Father of the poor!
Come, Source of all our store!
Come, within our bosoms shine!

Thou of comforters the best,
Thou the soul's most welcome guest,
Sweet refreshment here below!

In our labor, rest most sweet,
Grateful coolness in the heat,
Solace in the midst of woe!

O most blessed Light divine,
Fill our inmost hearts today;
Keep our natures free from sin,
Give us grace that we may win
Everlasting life one day.

Amen.

Daily Prayer for the Gifts

Holy Spirit, today I ask for the gift of
Wisdom — to see all things as You see them.

Give me Understanding to know Your truth.

Grant me Counsel to follow Your path.

Strengthen me with Fortitude for the journey.

Bless me with Knowledge of Your love.

Fill me with Piety to worship You worthily.

Keep me in Fear — holy reverence for Your name.

Amen.

The Twelve Fruits of the Holy Spirit

"But the fruit of the Spirit is love, joy, peace, patience, kindness, goodness, faithfulness, gentleness, self-control." — Galatians 5:22-23

💕

Love

Caritas

The greatest of all virtues, love is the selfless, unconditional care for others that reflects God's own love for humanity.

😊

Joy

Gaudium

A deep, abiding sense of happiness that comes from knowing and being united with God, regardless of external circumstances.

☮️

Peace

Pax

The tranquility of soul that comes from being in right relationship with God, others, and ourselves.

⏳

Patience

Patientia

The ability to endure difficulties and delays without becoming upset, trusting in God's timing.

🤝

Kindness

Benignitas

The generous inclination to do good to others, showing tenderness and consideration in our interactions.

🌟

Goodness

Bonitas

The moral excellence and uprightness in character that flows from a relationship with God.

🤲

Faithfulness

Fidelitas

Loyalty and reliability in keeping our commitments and promises, especially to God and others.

🕊️

Gentleness

Modestia

The virtue of being mild-mannered and patient, showing self-control and consideration for others.

🎯

Self-Control

Temperantia

The ability to govern and restrain our desires and passions, living according to God's will.
